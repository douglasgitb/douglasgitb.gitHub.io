convergence

 

Next was the dreaded convergence process. My theory about the rubber wedges seems to be working pretty well. I haven’t messed with it too much other than to get the rotation right. At first I used the corners of the box around the perimeter of the screen, but then I noticed the middle vertical line was stairstepping across the actual rows of phosphors on the face of the tube. So I switched to use that line as a reference, hovering over the top of the tube, looking down obliquely to foreshorten the length of the line, and rotated the yoke until I eliminated the aliasing and it worked very well.

Convergence was not perfect, but in the ballpark right off the bat. Turns out the original position of the purity rings was not going to work. So, following the procedure in the Wells-Gardner Quadrascan Color X-Y Display manual (TM183-3) I set about doing the convergence. Go step by step, don’t skip any steps, take your time, and as it states, concentrate on the central area of the tube.
