the transplant

 

I took some measurements of how far back the purity rings on both the TV and 6100 were from the yoke, just in case they might be needed. So far I haven’t used them, which is good because they weren’t the most precise. Next comes the careful removal of the purity rings and yoke from both tubes.

I left the wedges in place on the TV, figuring that it wouldn’t hurt to see if they were in the right spot for the new yoke. I think the alignment of each component (yoke, tube, electron gun, shadow mask, phosphor coating) varies from unit to unit, requiring manual positioning of the yoke and magnets, but it was worth a shot. I slid the yoke up snug to the wedges.
