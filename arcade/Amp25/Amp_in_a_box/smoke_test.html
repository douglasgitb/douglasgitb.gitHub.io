smoke test

 

The yoke was snug up against the undisturbed rubber wedges on the destination tube, with the thought that the unique geometric relationship between the electron guns in the neck and the shadow mask and phosphor coating inside each individual tube are what determines the required yoke spacing, and therefore the original wedges might be right even for a new yoke. Next came the purity rings, making sure they weren’t put on backwards. They were slid into the same position as the originals, as determined by the carefully uncleaned layer of dust on the neck. Most tube swap guides mention marking the relative position of the tabs sticking out of the rings, but I think the distance front to back is more important to preserve. I did preserve the position of the tabs, as well.

The TV cabinet makes a convenient place to mount the deflection and HV boards. On the left is my routing of the diode mods to the deflection board, avoiding attachment to the lead of the main transistors.

On the right is the initial power up of the tube, Gravitar in splendorific 25” Amplifone goodness (with low HV).
